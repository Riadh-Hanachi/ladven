About Us

LaDVEN (Laboratory for the Development of Water and Environmental Nanotechnologies) is a multidisciplinary research lab focused on water treatment, desalination, and environmental monitoring. Our team brings together experts in chemistry, materials science, and data analysis to tackle challenges related to water quality and sustainability. We aim to develop innovative solutions for safer and more accessible drinking water.

Research Areas

LaDVEN conducts interdisciplinary research to ensure access to safe and sustainable water resources through innovative technologies and environmental monitoring.

Desalination Technologies

Exploring and optimizing membrane-based and solar desalination systems for sustainable freshwater production.

Microplastics Monitoring

Detection and analysis of microplastics in drinking water sources and their impacts on health and ecosystems.

Water Quality Assessment

Application of advanced analytical methods to monitor physical, chemical, and biological indicators of water quality.

Computational Chemistry

Modeling molecular interactions in aqueous systems to support material design for water purification technologies.

AI & Data Science for Water

Use of machine learning and big data analytics to predict contamination patterns and optimize resource management.

Environmental Data Platforms

Development of open-access platforms to share environmental datasets and support collaborative research.

Our Partners

LaDVEN collaborates with national and international institutions to advance research in water quality, desalination, and environmental protection.

  • CBBC
  • Université de Carthage
  • Université de Sfax
  • EU Projects
  • Private Sector

CBBC (Centre de Biotechnologie de Borj-Cédria)

Key national partner in water research and desalination solutions.

Collaboration on coastal monitoring, plastic pollution analysis, and sustainable desalination technologies adapted to Tunisian needs.

Université de Carthage

Our academic home and main research support hub.

Joint supervision of theses, access to laboratory infrastructure, and coordination of water quality improvement projects.

Université de Sfax

Collaboration on membrane technology and chemical analysis.

Exchange of researchers, joint publications, and shared access to analytical instruments related to water treatment and microplastics studies.

European Projects

Active in several EU-funded research projects under Horizon Europe and PRIMA programs.

Projects focus on sustainable desalination, AI applications in water quality monitoring, and environmental risk management.

Private Sector & Startups

Partnerships with industrial actors for water treatment and filtration system development.

Innovation transfer, pilot system testing, and implementation of AI-enhanced sensors in collaboration with SMEs and green tech startups.
